# Ocean Rescue (WIP) - Master Game Design Document

> **Authoritative ruleset:** `docs/mechanics.md`. This document is the high-level vision/overview; for any
> precise rule, `mechanics.md` wins. Updated 2026-06-17 to match the locked decisions (26×26 grid, d12
> weather die, loot-every-tile movement, two-currency scoring, directional Winds + Joker, pure-paper
> table). See also `docs/design/gm-tools-ux.md` (tooling) and `docs/analysis/rules-review.md` (historical).

## 1. Overview
*   **Target Audience:** 12 teams of children (aged 8-14) at a 14-day summer camp.
*   **Theme:** Nautical exploration, rescuing animals, and solving navigational puzzles.
*   **Format:** A hybrid physical/offline game. A Game Master (GM) runs the game using heavily prepared, printed binders and materials generated by a custom toolsuite. 
*   **Win Condition:** The team with the most **Rescue Points (Punkty Ratunku)** at the end of the camp wins, while all teams collaborate to complete a shared "Camp Animal Album" (a separate cooperative goal).

## 2. Localization & Language Requirement (Strict)
*   **Internal Code:** Development, variables, and architectures will use English for maintainability.
*   **External Outputs:** **ALL** GM-facing user interfaces, printable PDFs, maps, puzzle instructions, TCG animal cards, and lookup tables MUST be generated and rendered entirely in **Polish**.

## 3. Core Components
*   **The Ocean Map:** A **26x26** grid representing the sea. Coordinates are letter-number (`A1`…`Z26`). Printed as a plain grid; tile values are static, secret, and randomly seeded (held only in the GM's lookup).
*   **Weather Die:** A custom **d12** (12 faces) — a WIP mix of Compass directions, "Still", and "Storm" (face counts tuned by the simulator).
*   **Winds (Resources):** Consumable **directional** tiles — **5 types**: Up, Down, Left, Right, and **Joker** (wildcard = any one direction when spent). Gained by **drawing at random** and recording them on the team score card. Spent **only** to solve puzzles.
*   **Archipelago Maps (Puzzles):** Printed sliding navigation puzzles (grids 5x5 up to 8x8). **Indexed**; reward scales with difficulty. **No solution/required moves are printed** on the sheet.
*   **Animal Cards:** **Unique, all-equal** collectible cards (no face value, no better/worse) featuring animal facts, organized by **category** (domestic / extinct / poisonous / …). Saved into the shared Album.

## 4. The Macro Layer: Ocean Exploration (Daily Loop)
The camp lasts ~14 days. The calendar is **living** (the GM may add turns ~1h ahead, around weather/
activities) but **every team always gets the same number of turns** — a hard fairness invariant. A
**turn** is one team's visit to the GM (one direction + one roll); inside it the ship makes several
**moves**.

1.  **Declare Direction:** The team selects one cardinal direction (N/E/S/W).
2.  **Roll the Weather Die (d12):** The face, relative to the declared direction, sets both the move count `X` (base 3, ±) and the heading: **tailwind** (rolled = declared) = far straight; **headwind** (opposite) = short straight; **crosswind** (perpendicular) = **diagonal**; **Storm** = far, each tile −1 loot; **Still** = short, each tile +2 loot.
3.  **Sail, Skip & Stop:** The ship advances in that heading, **jumping over visited tiles** (not counted) and **stopping on every unvisited tile**, up to `X` stops. At a grid wall it **ricochets** so the full move is always delivered — a team is **never stuck** (no Becalmed state).
4.  **Loot every stop:** **Each** unvisited tile stopped on is looted. The GM reads its static secret value, then adds the Storm/Still shift, the global day modifier, and any rubber-band bump live.
5.  **Global Modifiers:** The GM applies live, time-based modifiers (e.g., "+1 move" or "+N loot from Day 5").

## 5. The Micro Layer: Archipelago Puzzles
Loot tables frequently reward "Archipelago Maps" and "Winds". 

*   **Mechanic:** Puzzles use "Ice Cave" / "Ricochet Robot" movement. A ship slides continuously in a chosen direction until it hits an obstacle or wall.
*   **Cost to Solve:** Teams spend the **directional Winds** (incl. Joker wildcards) they actually use to execute a solution they work out themselves — nothing is printed on the map. Spent Winds are crossed off / returned to the GM.
*   **Holding & Juggling:** A team may hold **any number** of puzzles at once — **juggling a scarce, directional Wind supply across many open maps is the central strategic mechanic.**
*   **Turn-in & Reward:** Teams return solved maps any visit; the GM verifies against a secret solution key and grants the map's **indexed reward** (smaller/easier maps give less). Score gain per visit is **capped** (see §6), so hoarding-and-dumping can't spike the lead.

## 6. Economy & Scoring — two decoupled currencies
*   **Rescue Points (Punkty Ratunku) — competitive.** A written running number; most at camp end wins. Earned from tile stops + puzzle turn-ins, but **capped per visit** (overflow lost; Winds from those turn-ins are still kept). The cap tames hoard-and-dump *and* throttles early leaders (built-in catch-up).
*   **Rescued Animals (Uratowane Zwierzęta) — cooperative.** Unique, all-equal animal cards saved into the shared **Album**, **≤3 per visit**, organized by category. The deck running out is **harmless** (never blocks Rescue Points, never ends the game). Completing a **category** triggers a camp-wide milestone reward.
*   **Loot Hierarchy:** tile stops yield mostly **Winds and Archipelago Maps** plus some Rescue Points; **puzzle turn-ins** are the top source of Rescue Points and animal saves.
*   **Game end:** a **fixed, equal turn count** for all teams — never the deck or board running out.

## 7. Catch-up Mechanics (Rubber-Banding) — hybrid, not secret
*   **Rule-based baseline (printed):** the bottom-N teams get an automatic, gap-derived loot bump, printed on the GM's daily sheet — reproducible and dispute-proof.
*   **Bounded GM override (logged):** the GM may add a small extra bump **or an extra sail** (extra *moves* within a turn — never an extra *turn*, so equal-turns holds) for edge cases, and must log it.
*   The **per-visit Rescue-Point cap** already provides strong automatic catch-up; rubber-banding is a secondary tuning tool.

## 8. Tooling & Architecture Constraints
A browser-based Single-Page Application (SPA) generates the game *before* camp; the camp itself is run
**entirely on paper** (no screen or live tool at the table). The shared visited-set lives on a wall-
mounted **A1 printed grid with stickers**; tile values come from a **GM-only secret lookup**. A single
seed reproduces an identical binder + map. The SPA modules share core logic and state:
1.  **Ocean Map Generator:** the plain **26×26** A1 grid (no values) + the GM-only **secret static value table**; living-calendar baseline.
2.  **Puzzle Generator:** procedurally generates valid, solvable sliding puzzles, **indexed by difficulty**, with a GM solution key (no printed solution).
3.  **Reward/Loot Engine:** auto-balances the value→reward table from GM ratios (Winds vs. Puzzles vs. Animals vs. Rescue Points), sets the 5-type Wind draw pool (incl. Joker) and the per-visit Rescue-Point cap.
4.  **Economy Simulator:** Monte-Carlo of the camp — the *only* code that runs the full hand-played rules — checking Rescue-Point fairness, board never starving, Album completable, early-puzzle solvability.
5.  **TCG / Album Generator** *(already built)*: populates printable cards with AI animal facts; cards are **unique and flat**, grouped by **category**.
